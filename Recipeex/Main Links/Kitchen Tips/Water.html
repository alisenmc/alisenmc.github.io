Kitchen Tips


Use Pasta Water to Create a Pasta Sauce


Don't throw the water out after your pasta is cooked. It's a simple thing, but it might feel like a revelation. If you haven't heard this cooking motto before, consider adapting to it now. The water left in the pot after you have cooked your spaghetti, fusilli, or shells is loaded with the starch the pasta left behind, which is why it looks cloudy.

Pasta water works best when added to sauce made in a pan—that means noodles enjoyed with marinara sauce or Bolognese don't really have a need for the starchy cooking water. But should you find that your sauce is too thick, or that it isn't clinging to your pasta enough, instead of adding more oil use a little pasta water to thin it out. Knowing when to use pasta water just takes a bit of training.

Think a step ahead: Catch yourself before you drain the pasta in a colander, and ladle a cup or two of water from the pot. Even more efficient is the professional cook's technique: Skip the colander and scoop the cooked pasta with a mesh spider directly from the pot into a waiting skillet.
