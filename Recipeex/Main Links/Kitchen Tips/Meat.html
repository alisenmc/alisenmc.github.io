Why Do You Let Meat Rest?


It is important to let meat rest after cooking so it can reabsorb and distribute the juices that have been constricted during the cooking process.

If you were to cut a steak right off the grill, youâ€™ll notice that the internal juices pool out of the meat, leaving you with a dry and tough final product. The reason for this is because of what happens to the muscle fibers when they are heated:

When the steak or is heated, the muscle fibers constrict. This constriction pushes the juices in those fibers away from the heat source and towards the center of the meat. Since all of the moisture is concentrated in the center of the meat, it will pour out of the meat as soon as it is cut, making it look unappealing and bloody while taking the moisture and flavor with it. The steak ends up dry and flavorless.
